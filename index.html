📈 Capital Gains Key Rules
Capital Gain = Sale Price – Cost – Transfer Expenses
  • Grandfathering (112A): For equity pre-31-Jan-2018, cost = FMV on 31-Jan-2018
  • Indexation (CII): Available for property sold pre-Jul-2024 → 20% rate
  • STT Condition: 111A/112A special rates require STT payment
  • Loss Set-Off: STCL offsets STCG+LTCG; LTCL offsets only LTCG
  • Carry Forward: 8 years — must file ITR even if no tax
  • Bonus Shares: Cost = ₹0; date = allotment date

👥 Who Files ITR-2?
✅ Use ITR-2 if you have any of:
  • Salary > ₹50 lakh
  • Capital Gains (any amount)
  • Multiple house properties
  • Foreign assets / income
  • Agricultural income > ₹5,000
  • Director in company or unlisted shares
  • Lottery, horse race income
❌ Don't use ITR-2 for:
Business/profession income → ITR-3. For simple cases (salary ≤ ₹50L, one HP, no CG, no foreign) → ITR-1 (Sahaj)

⚠️ Common Errors & Pro Tips
Mistakes That Trigger Notices
  • Not reconciling with AIS (Annual Information Statement)
  • Missing dividend income — fully taxable post-2020
  • Not reporting LTCG even if below ₹1.25L — must fill Schedule CG
  • Missing Schedule FA for foreign accounts — penalty up to ₹10L
  • Claiming 80C/80D in New Regime — defective return notice
  • No Schedule VDA for crypto (even losses)
  • Using ITR-1 when CG exists — invalid return
💡 Pro Tips
  • Download AIS from income.tax.gov.in before filing
  • File by 31 July to avoid ₹5,000 late fee
  • E-verify within 30 days — else return is invalid
  • File even if tax = ₹0, to preserve capital loss carry-forward
  • Keep broker statements, Form 16, Form 26AS ready

🧠 Tax Smart Strategies
Tax Harvesting, Loss Carry-Forward & Legal Tax Minimisation
💡
These are 100% legal strategies built into the Income Tax Act. Using them is smart planning — not tax evasion.
🌾 LTCG Tax Harvesting — The ₹1.25L Free Harvest Every Year
₹1,25,000 LTCG is tax-free every year — if you don't use it, you lose it forever
Smart investors deliberately book ₹1.25L of equity LTCG every year to reset their cost basis, even if they plan to stay invested.
How It Works: Sell and Immediately Rebuy
Step 1: Identify equity MF / stocks held > 12 months with unrealised gains
Step 2: Sell units to book exactly ₹1.25L of LTCG
Step 3: Rebuy the same units next day at new (higher) price
Result: Cost basis resets upward — future gains calculated from new price. Gain permanently freed from tax!
WITHOUT Harvesting (sold after 3 yrs)
Cost 3 years ago₹5,00,000
Value now₹9,00,000
LTCG = ₹4L; exempt ₹1.25L; taxable ₹2.75L
Tax @ 12.5%₹34,375
WITH Harvesting (₹1.25L booked each year)
Year 1: Book ₹1.25L LTCG → rebuy₹0 tax
Year 2: Book ₹1.25L LTCG → rebuy₹0 tax
Year 3: Remaining gain is small — mostly exempt₹0 tax
Tax saved vs no harvesting~₹31,250 🎉
⚠️ Key Rules
  • No wash sale rule in India — unlike USA, you CAN sell and rebuy immediately
  • Holding period resets — rebought units restart fresh 12-month clock
  • Factor in costs: exit load (if any), STT, brokerage on both legs
  • Best timing: February–March before financial year closes
  • MF NAV risk: 1 day settlement gap — price may shift between sell and rebuy
📉 Loss Harvesting — Turn Losers Into Tax Savers
Book losses now to offset gains — reduce your current year tax bill
Sell losing positions to crystallise the loss, offset it against your gains, and reduce tax. You can immediately rebuy the same asset.

🚨 File ITR Even If No Tax Due This Year
Had losses but no gains to offset? Still file ITR by 31 July. That filing is what legally registers your loss carry-forward. Skip it and you lose the benefit permanently — even for ₹10L of losses.
⚡ 87A + Capital Gains Interaction — The Often Missed Opportunity
STCG/LTCG income does NOT affect your 87A rebate eligibility!
The ₹12L rebate limit checks slab income only. You can have unlimited LTCG/STCG on top and still get full 87A rebate on your salary portion.

Example: Salary ₹9.25L (net) + LTCG ₹10L = Total ₹19.25L
87A check: Slab income = ₹9.25L ≤ ₹12L ✅ → Slab tax = ₹0 via rebate
LTCG tax: (₹10L – ₹1.25L) × 12.5% = ₹1,09,375 only
Total tax: ~₹1.14L on ₹19.25L income

🏦 NPS 80CCD(2) — The Only Remaining Deduction, Maximise It
Ask your employer to restructure salary with NPS contribution component
Employer's NPS contribution u/s 80CCD(2) reduces taxable income — 14% of basic (Govt) / 10% (Private).

How: Employer reduces cash pay by ₹X, contributes ₹X to your NPS. CTC unchanged, taxable income drops.

Example: Basic ₹10L/yr → Employer NPS 10% = ₹1L → Tax saved at 15% slab = ₹15,600/year (incl. cess)

Trade-off: Locked till age 60. 60% corpus tax-free at maturity; 40% must buy annuity (annuity income is taxable).
📋 Year-End Tax Smart Checklist (February–March)
□ 1. Calculate LTCG used so far — How much of ₹1.25L exemption is utilised?
□ 2. Harvest remaining LTCG exemption — Sell + rebuy to use up to ₹1.25L
□ 3. Identify loss positions — Any stocks/MFs sitting at a loss?
□ 4. Book losses to offset gains — STCL offsets STCG+LTCG; LTCL offsets LTCG
□ 5. Check 87A rebate cliff — Is slab income near ₹12L? Avoid slab-rate income that pushes you over
□ 6. Check holding period before selling — Are you close to 12/24-month mark?
□ 7. Maximise employer NPS — Request 80CCD(2) restructuring if not done
□ 8. File ITR by 31 July — Even if no tax due, to preserve loss carry-forwards

D. Set-Off of Losses (Within Schedule CG)
The portal has a set-off worksheet within Schedule CG. Fill losses in this order:
1. Current year STCL → set off against current year STCG (111A first, then others)
2. Remaining STCL → set off against LTCG
3. Current year LTCL → set off against LTCG only
4. Brought-forward losses (from Schedule BFLA) → applied after current year losses
5. Any unabsorbed loss → goes to Schedule CFL for future carry-forward
E. Schedule BFLA — Brought Forward Loss Adjustment
If you had capital losses in previous years (carried forward in Schedule CFL of last ITR):
Enter them in Schedule BFLA — the portal will apply them against current year gains automatically.

What to fill: AY of loss, type (STCL/LTCL), amount brought forward. Get this from your last filed ITR's Schedule CFL.
